Countries:

Polynesia is the home to a vast range of people and islands. It is a triangular area that contains 25 clusters of islands, some of which would be: New Zealand, Tonga, the Cook Islands, Samoa, Niue, Wallis, Tuvalu, and French Polynesia

Environment

The Polynesian islands were formed by molten lava that seeped through cracks in the Earth's crust. The Polynesian islands are located near the equator, making the climate humid, hot, and tropical. Most of the plants and animals on and near the islands originated from Southeast Asia. These species slowly moved to the islands through the natural distribution seeds and the movement of humans. Settlers also brought edible plants like taro, pepper, bananas, and coconut palms.

Languages:

Due to a large amount of diverse people in Polynesia, there are many different languages spoken throughout the region. There are about 30 languages spoken, but the main ones are Samoan, Maori, Tahitian, and Hawaiian.

Food:

Polynesia boasts a wide range of foods. Some foods that the countries of Polynesia offer are:

Kalua Pua'a: Kalua Pua'a is roast pork, and is made in an underground steam oven (also called a Hawaiian Imu). It is usuallly seasoned with sea salt and green onions. It is usually eaten at most luaus.

Religion/Practices:

Polynesian religions are mainly based on the experience's people have had with the people they worship rather than on faith. Their experiences can include "encounters" with gods and spirits, both good and bad. For example, neglected souls can be seen becoming wandering spirits, and aborted fetuses can be turned into vicious demons. Other experiences of this can be when someone is on the verge of death. The soul of the dying person can warn the person's family, without the knowledge of the person. After the person's death, the person may visit friends and family.
